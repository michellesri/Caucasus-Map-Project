The Caucasus

East meets West. Old meets New. Mountain meets Plain.

At the intersection of language, culture, and politics,
the Caucasus region, named for the eponymous mountain range that divides it,
represents the nexus of six major world cultures.

Historical Richness

A Profoundly Diverse Region

Many empires have laid claim to the Caucasian region, from Greeks and Mongols to Byzantines, Ottomans, and Soviets. Between the successive waves of conquerors and the natural isolation of mountain communities, the Caucasus is extraordinarily diverse both linguistically and ethnically.

Linguistic Diversity

More than Sixty Living Languages

The Caucasus, an area scarcely larger than New England, is home to more than sixty languages and almost as many ethnic groups. These languages belong to six completely distinct linguistic families, with each family bearing almost no grammatical resemblance to the others.

Invisible Borders

Bounded by Religion, not Topography

The Caucasus is home to more than 50 different ethnic concentrations. They represent the geographic confluence of Europe and Asia with the Caucasian groups that have lived there for millennia, with religion as the defining factor.
